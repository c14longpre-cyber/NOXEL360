## Term / Concept
HTML Headings: H1, H2, H3, H4, H5, H6

## In one sentence (simple)
H1 to H6 are titles and subtitles that structure a webpage.

## Plain-language explanation (vulgarized)
Think of a book:
- H1 = the book title
- H2 = chapter titles
- H3 = sections inside a chapter
- H4–H6 = smaller subsections

On a webpage:
- H1 is usually the main title (often one per page).
- H2 breaks the page into major sections.
- H3 breaks H2 sections into smaller parts.

This helps both humans and search engines understand your content.

## Why it matters
Good heading structure improves:
- Accessibility (screen readers)
- SEO (content understanding)
- Readability and clarity

## Common mistakes
- Using multiple H1s without a clear structure.
- Skipping levels randomly (H1 → H4 → H2).
- Using headings just for visual style instead of structure.
